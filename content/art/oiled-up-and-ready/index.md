My greatest work of art!

It's OmegaFalcon covered in oil, staring at his nude body in the mirror. The concept was pretty innocuous to me at first, but I quickly realized how absurd and surreal it could actually be. I _really_ wanted to lean into that!

The GD icons are already always technically naked, they don’t wear clothes and don’t really have a body to cover up. So a cube standing naked could never even be recognized as such. I found it pretty ironic that to depict the character naked, I would have to **add** body parts to it. So I added two nice round butt cheeks, glistening in the oil. And that's when I ran into a line I felt compelled to cross.

C'mon, having a cube existing in stark nudity on the BACK side is just ***begging*** the question — what about the front? This question was so distracting to me I felt like it was egregiously taking away from what the piece was about. I didn’t set out to draw some juvinile piece, but the butt was drawing all the attention like a punchline as if that was the intended focus. The concept was supposed to be purely a guy **naked** and covered in oil looking at his reflection — in a very grounded, raw, and human way. Like an ancient Greek statue.

#### I drew the penis.

---

<br>
This image was very controversial when I first put it out on Twitter. Most people immediately perceived it as some gross, offensive, and perverted Geometry Dash pornography.

That kind of reaction was something I was eager to see tho, because it actually serves as part of the message of the piece.

_"Is this porn? Well, do you think the penis is sexy? No? Then why do you assume it's supposed to be?"_

I intentionally drew the penis with no detail, it's just lie simple droop. It's totally flaccid, there's isn't an air of eroticism at all. Yet people couldn't help being offended by whay they percieved as GD rule34.

<img src="/images/gdrule34.png" width="100%" alt="twitter screenshot complainng about gd porn">

The way I drew the penis reminds me of how penises are drawn on some child cartoon characters like Cartman from South Park or Goku from Dragon Ball. People who see it for the first time are often caught off guard, and maybe a few become offended at the idea of these characters being “sexualized”. But inclusion of a penis in these instances is never meant to arouse or be erotic and the fans of these franchises know that full well, so they get a good laugh out of it. Considering these characters are children, it would actually be exceedingly offensive if it was meant to be erotic. But you know it’s not supposed to be due to the context and way its presented. No detail, no focus, just small and realistically mundane. It’s really not there as some perverted fan service.

I was interested in how many people would jump to the conclusion that my art was pornographic just because of the inclusion of the penis, even when it’s presented completely non-erotically. I think it says a lot about them that they do. Or about society, right? Why is the idea of the character being naked, and the inclusion of buttocks and oil not a problem, but a little droopy flaccid penis is? I think people assumed my **intentions** were perverse and so they perceived my art with reproach.

If you remove the lens of penis = porn, the art simply becomes a geometry dash cube looking at himself nude in the mirror covered in oil. Why? He’s not showing off his junk, he doesn’t seem to be admiring his figure. Why is he covered in oil? What’s he doing after this? What's he _ready_ for?

**It’s absurd, it’s surreal, it’s silly, it’s beautiful. It’s simple and dumb and yet it invoked strong reactions in people afraid of my intentions. What a masterpiece!**
